Academic Integrity

Beyond GPA requirements, Beta Theta Pi members are expected to uphold the highest standards of academic integrity.

Prohibited Conduct

  • Cheating: Using unauthorized materials or assistance on exams or assignments
  • Plagiarism: Presenting another's work as your own without proper citation
  • Fabrication: Falsifying data, citations, or research results
  • Facilitating Academic Dishonesty: Helping others cheat or providing unauthorized assistance

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What is the All-Men's Average (AMA)?

A: The AMA is the average cumulative GPA of all male students at Samford University. It is calculated each semester by the university.

Q: When are GPAs checked?

A: GPAs are reviewed at the end of each semester when official grades are posted. The Scholarship Chair tracks all members' academic standing.

Q: Can I still participate in events if I'm on Academic Warning?

A: Yes. Members on Academic Warning retain full membership privileges but must complete required study hours and check-ins.

Q: What happens if I improve my GPA?

A: If your GPA improves to meet the chapter standard, you immediately return to Good Academic Standing and requirements are lifted.

Q: Who should I contact if I'm struggling academically?

A: Reach out to the Scholarship Chair first. They can connect you with tutoring, study resources, and support. The Vice President of Brotherhood and chapter advisors are also available to help.
