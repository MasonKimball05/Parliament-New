Election Overview

Eligibility Requirements

General Requirements (All Officers)

  • Must be an Active Member in good standing
  • Minimum 2.5 cumulative GPA (some positions require higher)
  • No outstanding Kai cases or sanctions
  • Financially current (no unpaid dues or fees)
  • Able to serve full one-year term
  • Complete officer training requirements

Position-Specific GPA Requirements

Higher GPA Required (2.7+):

  • Treasurer

Highest GPA Required (3.0+):

  • Scholarship Chair

Experience Preferences

  • President: Prior Executive Board service strongly preferred
  • EVP: Understanding of Robert's Rules required
  • All positions: Demonstrated leadership and commitment to chapter

Slating Process Timeline

The slating and election process typically occurs over 4-6 weeks in the spring semester.

Week
1-2

Announcement & Application Period

  • President announces election timeline
  • Position descriptions distributed to membership
  • Application forms made available
  • Current officers host informational sessions
  • Members encouraged to shadow current officers
  • Application deadline set (typically 2 weeks)
Week
3

Candidate Review & Interviews

  • Executive Board reviews all applications
  • Verify eligibility (GPA, conduct, finances)
  • Schedule candidate interviews
  • Conduct individual interviews with each candidate
  • Assess qualifications and readiness
  • Request references if needed
Week
4

Slate Creation & Presentation

  • Executive Board deliberates on best candidates
  • Create proposed slate of officers
  • Consider team dynamics and balance
  • Present slate to chapter at meeting
  • Allow time for questions about slate
  • Open floor for additional nominations
Week
5

Campaigning & Candidate Speeches

  • Candidates may campaign to membership
  • One-on-one conversations encouraged
  • Attend chapter events and meetings
  • Prepare speech for election meeting (3-5 minutes)
  • Address qualifications and vision
  • Positive campaigning only (no attacks on opponents)
Week
6

Election Meeting & Vote

  • Special chapter meeting for elections
  • All candidates give speeches
  • Q&A session with membership
  • Secret ballot voting
  • Vote counting by impartial tellers
  • Results announced immediately

Voting Procedures

Slate Voting (Preferred Method)

Vote on entire slate of officers as a package:

  • Single vote: Accept or Reject slate
  • Requires 2/3 majority to pass
  • Faster and more efficient
  • Ensures balanced leadership team

If slate passes: All officers elected immediately

If slate fails: Vote on individual positions

Individual Position Voting

Vote on each position separately:

  • One position at a time
  • Multiple candidates possible per position
  • Requires majority to win
  • Runoff if no majority achieved

Used when: Slate fails 3 times OR only one position contested

Post-Election Procedures

Immediate Actions (Within 48 hours)

  • Results announced to membership
  • Election results reported to General Fraternity
  • Updated roster submitted to university
  • Transition timeline established

Transition Period (2-4 weeks)

  • Elected officers shadow current officers
  • Access to files, accounts, and resources transferred
  • Attend mandatory officer training
  • Meet with advisors and General Fraternity staff
  • Receive position-specific training
  • Develop goals and plans for term

Installation & Oath

  • Formal installation ceremony
  • Officers take oath of office
  • Recognition from chapter and alumni
  • Official term begins
