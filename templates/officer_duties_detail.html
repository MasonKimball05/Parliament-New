The 9 Executive Board Officers (Constitution Article III)

About the Executive Board

The Executive Board consists of 9 elected officers who lead the Alpha Mu chapter. Each officer has specific responsibilities outlined in the Constitution and Bylaws.

General Officer Requirements

  • Attend annual officer transition meeting
  • Attend all required campus and General Fraternity events/trainings
  • Attend regularly scheduled Chapter and Executive Board meetings
  • Prepare and give officer reports at each meeting
  • Understand and abide by Code of Beta, Risk Management Policy, Chapter Constitution & Bylaws, and IFC policies
  • Serve as role model within chapter and campus community

P

President

Chief Executive Officer of the Chapter

Detailed duties and responsibilities are specified in the Constitution and Bylaws.

Core Responsibilities

  • Preside over all chapter and Executive Board meetings
  • Act as official representative of the chapter
  • Serve as primary liaison with General Fraternity
  • Coordinate with chapter advisors and house corporation
  • Lead strategic planning and goal setting
  • Ensure chapter compliance with all policies
  • Appoint committee chairs (with board approval)
  • Facilitate conflict resolution

Additional Duties

  • Create meeting agendas
  • Call special meetings when necessary
  • Sign official chapter documents
  • Represent chapter at IFC and university events
  • Mentor other executive officers
  • Oversee officer transition process
  • Make executive decisions in emergencies
  • Submit required reports to General Fraternity

üîó Related Procedures

See Robert's Rules: Chair's Duties

Qualifications: Must have served on Executive Board for at least one semester. Strong leadership skills, organizational ability, and comprehensive understanding of chapter operations required.

EVP

Executive Vice President & IFC Delegate

Parliamentarian & Second-in-Command

Detailed duties and responsibilities are specified in the Constitution and Bylaws.

Core Responsibilities

  • Serve as chapter parliamentarian
  • Assume President's duties in their absence
  • Enforce Robert's Rules of Order
  • Conduct Robert's Rules training (minimum once per term)
  • Oversee chapter bylaws and constitution
  • Manage amendment process
  • Serve as IFC Delegate
  • Advise on parliamentary procedure

Additional Duties

  • Rule on points of order during meetings
  • Review proposed amendments for compliance
  • Train new members on meeting procedures
  • Assist President with meeting management
  • Coordinate with General Fraternity on governance
  • Maintain official copy of governing documents
  • Represent chapter at IFC meetings

üîó Related Procedures

See Robert's Rules of Order

Qualifications: Must have strong understanding of Robert's Rules of Order and parliamentary procedure. Previous board experience preferred. Must be able to remain impartial and enforce rules fairly.

VPA

Vice President of Administration

Record Keeper & Chapter Secretary

Detailed duties and responsibilities are specified in the Constitution and Bylaws.

  • Record and maintain meeting minutes per Robert's Rules standards
  • Manage chapter correspondence
  • Maintain attendance records
  • Distribute meeting agendas and minutes
  • Archive official chapter documents
  • Track member contact information
  • Count and record all votes
  • Ensure absences are submitted 24 hours in advance

VPB

Vice President of Brotherhood

Kai Committee Chair & Member Development

Detailed duties and responsibilities are specified in the Constitution and Bylaws.

Core Responsibilities

  • Chair the Kai Committee
  • Plan and execute brotherhood events
  • Foster positive chapter culture
  • Manage member conduct issues
  • Coordinate Big Brother/Little Brother program
  • Oversee new member education
  • Plan social activities and bonding events
  • Address interpersonal conflicts

Additional Duties

  • Convene Kai Committee meetings
  • Contact members referred to Kai
  • Maintain Kai Committee records
  • Organize semester brotherhood retreat
  • Plan weekly brotherhood activities
  • Facilitate Big/Little matching process
  • Track member satisfaction and morale
  • Coordinate with advisors on member development

üîó Related Procedures

See Kai Committee Procedures

Qualifications: Strong interpersonal skills and emotional intelligence required. Must be trusted and respected by membership. Experience in conflict resolution and mediation preferred.

VPF

Vice President of Finance

Financial Officer

Detailed duties in Constitution & Bylaws

  • Manage chapter finances and budget
  • Collect dues and fees via Billhighway
  • Pay bills and manage accounts
  • Present financial reports at meetings
  • Oversee fundraising activities
  • Maintain financial records
  • Coordinate with house corporation on finances
  • Prepare annual budget with Finance Committee
GPA Requirement: Must maintain 2.7+ GPA
VPRM

Vice President of Risk Management

Safety & Policy Compliance

Detailed duties in Constitution & Bylaws

  • Ensure event safety compliance
  • Educate on Beta Risk Management Policy
  • Monitor alcohol and substance policies
  • Coordinate with university officials
  • Manage event sober monitors
  • Review all chapter events for risk
  • Ensure chapter follows all safety protocols
Works closely with: President, Advisors, Risk Management Committee
VPR

Vice President of Recruitment

Membership Growth

Detailed duties in Constitution & Bylaws

  • Lead recruitment strategy and planning
  • Organize rush events
  • Coordinate with IFC on recruitment
  • Manage potential new member outreach
  • Train recruitment team
  • Track recruitment metrics
  • Coordinate with Alumni for recruitment support
  • Lead Recruitment Committee (5-9 members)
Works closely with: Rush Chair, President, Recruitment Advisor
VPE

Vice President of Education

New Member Education & Development

Detailed duties in Constitution & Bylaws

  • Lead Standard New Member Orientation
  • Coordinate annual member education programs
  • Oversee new member development
  • Ensure compliance with General Fraternity education requirements
  • Plan educational workshops and seminars
  • Track new member progress
  • Lead Education Committee
Works closely with: Vice President of Brotherhood, Education Advisor

Vice President of Programming

Events, Service & Activities

  • Plan and coordinate service events
  • Organize philanthropy activities
  • Coordinate intramural sports teams
  • Plan special chapter events
  • Track service hours for members
  • Lead Programming Committee
  • Coordinate with campus organizations
Works closely with: President, Social Chair, Programming Committee
