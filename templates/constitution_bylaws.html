Constitution

Article I: Name and Purpose

Section 1. Name

The name of this organization shall be the Samford chapter, the Alpha Mu of Beta Theta Pi.

Section 2. Purpose

The purpose of this chapter is to uphold the values, principles, and standards of Beta Theta Pi as outlined in the fraternity's Constitution and Code.

Code of Beta Theta Pi (44th Edition) - The fraternity's governing document that this chapter follows.

Article II: Membership

Section 1. Membership Requirements

  • Eligible members must be regularly enrolled male students, either graduate or undergraduate, at Samford University
  • Members must not belong to any similar fraternity or organization

Section 2. Membership Categories

  • Collegiate Member: As defined in the Code of Beta Theta Pi, Chapter VIII Section 1(B)(a)
  • Active Member: A Collegiate Member who is neither a Temporarily Inactive Member nor a Suspended Member
  • Temporarily Inactive Member: As defined in the Code of Beta Theta Pi, Chapter VIII Section 1(B)(b)
  • Alumnus Member: As defined in the Code of Beta Theta Pi, Chapter VIII Section 1(B)(c)
  • Suspended Member: As defined in the Code of Beta Theta Pi, Chapter VIII Section 1(B)(d)
  • Recused Member: As defined in the Code of Beta Theta Pi, Chapter VIII Section 1(B)(e)

Section 3. Membership Procedures

  • All new members must be initiated according to the General Fraternity's initiation Ritual and the chapter's pre-initiation rituals
  • Membership rights are equal for all members, regardless of race, color, creed, religion, age, disability, ethnic orientation, sexual orientation, national origin, or position within the fraternity or chapter

Section 4. Membership Records

The chapter must maintain an official roll book of all initiated members, which should include each member's first name, last name, initiation date, roll number, and date of birth.

Article III: Leadership

Section 1. Introduction

An Executive Board will serve as leaders and officers. These members are responsible for all operational activities and facilitate the development of a positive chapter culture congruent with the values of Beta Theta Pi.

Section 2. Purpose of the Executive Board

  • Facilitate the strategic vision and sustainable operation of the chapter
  • Control financial assets
  • Report at stated times and intervals on the condition of the chapter
  • Prepare business resulting in orderly and succinct meetings
  • Determine policy in advance for approval at chapter meetings
  • Provide in all other ways possible for the business-like administration of chapter affairs and policies

Article IV: Meetings

üîó Connection to Robert's Rules

Section 4 states: "All meetings are to be governed by Robert's Rules of Order."

Section 1. Regular Meetings

  • Weekly chapter meetings will be held at a set time to be established no later than the final weekly meeting of the proceeding semester
  • Executive Board meetings shall be scheduled at the discretion of the Executive Board and should generally be open to all chapter members

Section 2. Special Meetings

Special meetings may be called with four (4) hours advance notice by:

  • The President
  • The Executive Board
  • The advisory team
  • A supermajority of active, good standing members of the chapter

New Business introduced at a chapter meeting may not be voted upon until a subsequent meeting, unless two-thirds of the active members present and eligible to vote deem it necessary.

Section 3. Quorum

A quorum for chapter meetings shall consist of a simple majority of Active Members.

A quorum is required to pass any legislation at any special meetings or chapter meetings.

Section 4. Meeting Processes

1. All meetings are to be governed by Robert's Rules of Order

See our Robert's Rules Reference for detailed procedures on making motions, voting, and parliamentary procedure.

2. Meetings are closed to the public unless there are specific matters that permit non-members or non-pledges to attend.

Section 5. Voting

  • All present Active Members in Good Standing have one vote
  • The Vice President of Administration will count and record all votes in the minutes
  • An advisor of the chapter must be present for elections, amendments, Trial by Chapters, and selection of potential new members

Article VI: Amendments to the Constitution

Section 1. Proposals

  • Proposed amendments must be submitted in writing to the Executive Board for initial approval
  • Requires supermajority vote from the Executive Board
  • If Executive Board approval fails, member may bring motion to chapter meeting
  • Failed proposals cannot be reintroduced for 15 chapter meetings

Section 2. Approval of Amendments

Amendments to the constitution must be approved by a supermajority (2/3) of the active members present at a chapter or special meeting where a quorum is present.

Section 3. Amendment Authority

All amendments are subordinate to:

  • The Code of Beta Theta Pi
  • The Interfraternity Council
  • Samford University
  • Any applicable municipal, state, or federal laws

Bylaws

Article I: Membership

Section 1. Membership Expectations

The Five Pillars of Beta Membership:

  • Cultivation of the Intellect:
    • Maintain GPA at or above All Men's Average or 3.0 (whichever is higher) - See Academic Standards Guide
    • No cheating, plagiarism, or academic dishonesty
  • Responsible Conduct:
    • Urbane in deportment, courteous in expression, steadfast in friendship
    • No illegal or controlled substances
    • No alcohol or drug abuse
    • Follow all laws and Beta Risk Management Policy
  • Mutual Assistance:
    • Complete at least 15 hours of service per academic year
  • Integrity:
    • Uphold Beta standards even if campus culture is lower
    • No alcohol during PNM events or interactions
    • Substance and alcohol-free fraternity house
    • Meet all financial obligations in timely manner
  • Trust:
    • No hazing (as defined by Beta and Samford University)
    • Treat others with respect

Section 4. Good Standing

A member shall be considered in Good Standing unless such member:

  • Is deemed otherwise through the Kai Committee
  • Fails to meet requirements for Good Academic Standing
  • Fails to meet all fraternity financial obligations

Article II: Officers

Officer Slating Process

Timeline:

  • Selection occurs between fall initiation and end of fall semester
  • Class Representative Elections first

Slating Committee Membership:

  • President (chair)
  • Constitution and Bylaws Chair
  • One representative from each class (Senior, Junior, Sophomore, New Member)
  • Advisors (advisory vote for tie-breaking only)

Article IV: Financial

Dues & Fees

  • Annual dues set by chapter (majority vote required)
  • Payment plans available upon request to VP of Finance
  • All fees distributed via Billhighway
  • 2 weeks to pay in full or make first payment
  • Delinquent members referred to Kai Committee

Budget

  • Prepared by VP of Finance with Finance Committee and President
  • Must be approved by Finance Advisor and Chapter Counselor
  • Chapter approval by simple majority required
  • Must be approved prior to final chapter meeting of preceding term

Spending

  • All bills paid by check or Billhighway card
  • Funding requests submitted 48 hours in advance
  • Requires approval from 2 of: VP Finance, President, Finance Advisor, or Chapter Counselor

Article V: Rituals and Ceremonies

Section 1. Formal Chapter

  • Formal Chapter meetings must be held at least once a month
  • Follow Ritual outline for formal meetings
  • Badge Attire required (including tie and badge)
  • First meeting of each month should be designated as Formal Chapter
  • Opening and closing chapter meetings of each semester should be formal

Section 2. Pre-Initiation Practices

All pre-initiation and initiation ceremonies shall follow the fraternity's prescribed Ritual and be conducted within the chapter house or hall unless prior approval is obtained.

Section 3. Ceremony Approval

Local ritual ceremonies, not stated in the ritual book, must be submitted in writing to the District Chief for pre-approval.

Section 3: Definition of Common Terms

Good Standing

Members in "Good Standing" are eligible to vote, hold office, and enjoy other membership privileges. Requirements:

  • Financial Standing: All dues, fines, fees paid
  • Conduct: Adheres to all rules, no outstanding Kai cases
  • Participation: Active participation in chapter events, meetings, activities
  • Academic Standing: Meets or exceeds required GPA

Quorum

The number of members required to pass legislation. A quorum is a simple majority of active, Good Standing members within the chapter.

Supermajority

Two-Thirds (67%) of all members present. For voting, this is two-thirds of all eligible voting members of the chapter present.
