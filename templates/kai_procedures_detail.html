About the Kai Committee

The Kai Committee is the chapter's judicial body responsible for adjudicating breaches of membership expectations and acting as a mediating body for conflicts and conduct issues.

Violation Categories & Sanctions

Severe Violations

Potential for expulsion via Trial by Chapter

Examples:

  • Illegal drug use or distribution
  • Hazing violations
  • Felony criminal charges
  • DUI/DWI
  • Alcohol possession on chapter property
  • Sexual misconduct
  • Assault or violence

Potential Sanctions:

  • Expulsion via Trial by Chapter
  • Indefinite suspension from chapter activities
  • Referral to university conduct board
  • Loss of housing privileges

Moderate Violations

Suspension, probation, or removal from office

Examples:

  • Risk management policy violations
  • Failure to fulfill official duties
  • Property destruction or vandalism
  • Minor in possession (MIP) citation
  • Violation of university policies
  • Disruptive behavior at events

Potential Sanctions:

  • Removal from officer or chair position
  • Suspension from social events (1-6 months)
  • Social probation
  • Fines ($100-$500)
  • Community service hours
  • Mandatory counseling or education

Minor Violations

Warnings, fines, or work duty

Examples:

  • Excessive absences from required events
  • Unpaid dues or fees
  • Minor property damage
  • Disrespectful behavior
  • Failure to complete work duty
  • Dress code violations

Potential Sanctions:

  • Written or verbal warning
  • Minor fines ($25-$100)
  • Additional work duty hours
  • Letter of apology
  • Educational requirements

Kai Committee Process

1

Referral to Kai

Member is referred to Kai Committee by:

  • Executive Board member
  • Committee chair
  • Any chapter member with evidence
  • Self-referral
2

Notification

Kai Chair contacts the referred member:

  • Written notification of charges
  • Scheduled hearing date/time
  • Opportunity to prepare defense
  • Right to bring witnesses
3

Hearing

Formal Kai Committee hearing:

  • Presentation of evidence
  • Member's statement and defense
  • Witness testimony (if applicable)
  • Committee questions
  • Closed deliberation
4

Decision

Committee votes on findings and sanctions:

  • Majority vote determines guilt/innocence
  • Sanctions determined if guilty
  • Written decision provided to member
  • Report filed with Executive Board
5

Appeals Process

Member may appeal decision:

  • Written appeal to Executive Board within 7 days
  • Board reviews case and Kai decision
  • Board may uphold, reduce, or overturn sanctions
  • Board decision is final

Member Rights in Kai Proceedings

Before the Hearing

  • Written notification of specific charges
  • Reasonable time to prepare defense (minimum 48 hours)
  • Access to evidence against them
  • Right to request postponement (once, for good cause)

During the Hearing

  • Present their side of the story
  • Bring witnesses to testify
  • Question evidence presented
  • Have an advisor present (non-voting)
  • Fair and impartial committee

After the Decision

  • Receive written decision with explanation
  • Right to appeal to Executive Board
  • Confidentiality of proceedings
  • Opportunity for future reinstatement (if suspended)

General Protections

  • Presumption of innocence
  • No double jeopardy (same offense)
  • Protection from retaliation
  • Confidential records

Special Procedures

Academic Performance Issues

Members failing to meet GPA requirements are subject to automatic sanctions:

GPA Level Automatic Sanctions
Level 1: 2.5-2.7 5 study hours/week, Scholarship Chair meeting
Level 2: 2.3-2.49 8 study hours/week, Kai meeting, academic office referral
Level 3: 2.0-2.29 12 study hours/week, mandatory tutoring, social restrictions
Below 2.0: Automatic Kai referral with expulsion recommendation

Financial Delinquency

Members with outstanding dues or fees:

  • Referred to Treasurer for payment plan
  • If unresolved after 30 days, referred to Kai Committee
  • Possible suspension of privileges until paid
  • Cannot vote in elections or hold office while delinquent
